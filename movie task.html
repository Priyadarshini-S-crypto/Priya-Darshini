Plot Overview

Gautham (Suriya) and Kundavi (Jyothika) enter an arranged marriage despite personal reservations. Six years later, living in Mumbai with their five-year-old daughter, Kundavi finds Gautham’s college diary and learns he was once deeply in love with a girl named Aishwarya (Bhumika Chawla). Curious and distrustful, Kundavi invites Aishwarya to spend a day with her husband to fulfill Gautham’s unspoken wish. During this day, Kundavi imagines them rekindling their romance, but ultimately, Gautham realizes his love for Kundavi is genuine. Aishwarya gracefully steps aside after confirming his heart lies with his wife. The film ends with the family reunited, celebrating Diwali in harmony

Themes & characters

Kundavi :
Portrayed as a cheerful small-town girl forced into a loveless arranged marriage. Over time, she matures emotionally and reacts selflessly when faced with the past. Her decision to reunite her husband with his college love, even temporarily, reflects her depth and resilience.
Gautham :
Introduced as a brooding college rowdy who transforms into a responsible husband and father. The film charts his emotional journey—from youthful passion with Aishwarya to rediscovering contentment in his marriage with Kundavi.
Aishwarya (Aishu) :
Mysterious and emotionally rich, Aishwarya’s presence forces the central couple to confront unresolved feelings. Unlike the others, her character lingers in the viewer’s mind—symbolizing love never fulfilled and the inevitable acceptance of letting go.

Music & Soundtrack

The music, composed by A. R. Rahman, is widely praised—especially tracks like "Munbe Vaa", "Kummi Adi", and "New York Nagaram". The soundtrack is often described as a standout feature of the film, with the music elevating key emotional sequences and mood transitions

Reception

Audience views:
Many viewers found the storyline heartfelt and evocative. On Rotten Tomatoes, fans praised its dual exploration of premarital and post-marital love, calling it a “perfect blend of love”
Critic & Review Feedback:
While the music, performances, and technical aspects like cinematography and editing received accolades, some critics pointed to a predictable screenplay and cliched treatment, noting that the film resorts to formulaic storytelling and lacks deeper emotional engagement in parts
